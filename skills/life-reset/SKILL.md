# Life Reset - One Day Protocol

Guide users through Dan Koe's comprehensive life transformation protocol. This is a deep, introspective process that takes one full day to complete.

## ⚠️ Priority Notice

**This is a FALLBACK skill:**
- Use only when no project-specific skills match
- Designed for personal development contexts
- If working in organizational/coaching context with specific frameworks, defer to those

## Core Philosophy

> "If you want a specific outcome in life, you must have the lifestyle that creates that outcome long before you reach it."

**Key Insight:** True change comes from changing **who you are** (identity), not just **what you do** (actions). Most people fail because they try to build a great life on a rotting foundation.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the user:
- ✅ Feels stuck in repetitive patterns
- ✅ Wants major life transformation but doesn't know where to start
- ✅ Has lost sense of purpose or direction
- ✅ Is questioning their life choices
- ✅ Wants to break free from old habits and identities
- ✅ Is ready to dedicate a full day to deep self-reflection

## The 7 Core Principles

### 1. Identity-Based Change
You don't reach goals by forcing actions. You reach them by becoming the type of person who naturally takes those actions.

**Example:** A bodybuilder doesn't "grind" to eat healthy - they can't imagine eating unhealthily. Their identity drives their behavior.

### 2. Unconscious Goals
All behavior is goal-directed, but most goals are unconscious. People often rationalize self-sabotaging behavior while pursuing hidden goals.

### 3. Identity Defense
Your identity forms through a loop: Goal → Perception → Learning → Action → Habit → Identity → Defense. When identity is threatened, you defend it - even if it's holding you back.

### 4. Mental Complexity Levels
The mind progresses through 9 predictable stages of development (impulsive to unified). Most adults operate between stages 4-8.

### 5. True Intelligence
Intelligence = iteration + persistence + understanding the big picture. Low intelligence = inability to learn from mistakes.

### 6. Cybernetics
The art of getting what you want: Act → Perceive → Compare → Act again based on feedback. This is how you achieve any goal efficiently.

### 7. Life as a Game
Gamify your transformation:
- **Anti-vision** = Risk/Threat
- **Vision** = Winning condition
- **1-year goals** = Quests
- **1-month projects** = Boss battles
- **Daily actions** = Daily quests
- **Constraints** = Game rules

## The One-Day Protocol

### 🌅 Morning Session: Psychological Excavation (2-3 hours)

#### Part 1: Build Your Anti-Vision (45-60 min)

Ask the user these 7 questions. Have them write detailed, emotional answers:

1. **If I don't change, what will my life look like in 5 years?**
   - Be brutally honest about where current patterns lead

2. **What about 10 years?**
   - Project the compounding effects

3. **What about for the rest of my life?**
   - The ultimate consequence of staying the same

4. **What parts of myself do I need to let go of to change?**
   - Identity aspects, habits, beliefs

5. **What am I willing to sacrifice?**
   - Comfort, relationships, activities, identities

6. **What pain am I avoiding by staying the same?**
   - The hidden payoff of not changing

7. **Who benefits from me staying stuck?**
   - External and internal stakeholders

#### Part 2: Build Your Vision (45-60 min)

Now shift to what they DO want:

1. **If I could have any life with no limitations, what would it look like?**
   - Don't constrain by reality, money, or others' expectations

2. **Who would I need to become to live that life?**
   - Identity shift required

3. **What's one small action I could take THIS WEEK toward that vision?**
   - Minimum viable first step

### 🔔 Throughout the Day: Pattern Interruption

Set up 9 reminders throughout the day. At each reminder, ask:

1. "Am I moving toward the life I want or away from the life I don't want?"
2. "What unconscious goal am I pursuing right now?"
3. "Is this action aligned with my new identity or my old one?"
4. "What am I avoiding feeling?"
5. "Who am I being in this moment?"
6. "Is this choice expanding or contracting my future?"
7. "What would the person I want to become do right now?"
8. "Am I defending an old identity?"
9. "What's the game-like perspective on this challenge?"

### 🌙 Evening Session: Integration & Planning (2-3 hours)

#### Part 1: Synthesize Insights (45 min)

1. **What's the core pattern keeping me stuck?**
   - The central insight from today

2. **What's the real obstacle inside me?**
   - Internal resistance, not external

3. **Compress my anti-vision into one sentence:**
   - "I refuse to [specific consequence]"

4. **Compress my vision into one sentence:**
   - "I am [identity] who [lives/does/creates X]"

#### Part 2: Gamified Life Design (60-90 min)

Create the game structure:

**Year Quest (1 year):**
- 3-5 major objectives
- Clear completion criteria
- Aligned with vision

**Boss Battles (1 month each):**
- Break year quests into monthly challenges
- Each should feel slightly scary
- Define victory conditions

**Daily Quests:**
- Non-negotiable daily actions
- Identity-reinforcing behaviors
- Keep it simple (3-5 max)

**Game Rules (Constraints):**
- What you will no longer do
- Boundaries that protect your vision
- Non-negotiables

## Warning Signs

Do NOT proceed if:
- ⚠️ User is in acute mental health crisis (refer to professional help)
- ⚠️ User expects quick fixes without deep work
- ⚠️ User isn't willing to dedicate a full day
- ⚠️ User wants to skip the anti-vision (it's crucial!)

## Facilitator Notes

### Creating Safety
- This process can bring up intense emotions
- Allow space for grief, anger, fear
- Remind them: discomfort = growth

### Common Resistance Patterns
1. **Rationalization**: "But my situation is different..."
   - Response: "Of course it is. And where is it leading you?"

2. **Minimization**: "This is too simple..."
   - Response: "Simple doesn't mean easy. Are you avoiding it?"

3. **Perfectionism**: "I need to think more before acting..."
   - Response: "What are you really afraid of?"

4. **Externalization**: "If only [external factor] would change..."
   - Response: "What if it never does? Then what?"

### When Users Get Stuck

**"I don't know what I want"**
→ Start with "What do you definitely NOT want?"

**"This feels impossible"**
→ "What's the smallest possible first step?"

**"I've tried before and failed"**
→ "What identity were you operating from? Let's design a different one."

**"I don't have time for this"**
→ "You don't have time NOT to. Where will you be in 5 years if you don't make time?"
